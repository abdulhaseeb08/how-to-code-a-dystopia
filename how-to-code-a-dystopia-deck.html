SAY: First, the real history, because the tech matters. Mass surveillance didn't start with the iPhone. The US has been at this for a century, from a codebreaking 'Black Chamber' in 1919, to the NSA being founded in 1952 by a classified memo most of the government didn't know existed. In the 70s the Church Committee revealed they'd been wiretapping civil rights leaders and anti-war activists, no warrants. After 9/11 the PATRIOT Act made bulk collection legal-ish. Then PRISM let the NSA pull directly from the big tech platforms. And in 2013 Snowden showed us the scope: not suspects, everyone. The key technical shift: they stopped tapping individual lines and started collecting the metadata of entire populations. From wiretap to dataveillance.

You don't need a boot to abolish the individual. You just need a recommendation engine good enough that choosing feels like friction.
